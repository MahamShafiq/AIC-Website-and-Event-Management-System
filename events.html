Python For Data Scientists

12th July - 13th July, 2018

AI Club is proud to announce its event for the month of July 2018 - Python for Data Science. This workshop is a phenomenal opportunity for anyone interested in jumpstarting their Data Science journey with a comprehensive, hands-on introduction to one of Data Science's most important tools - the Python programming language. As a participant, you will learn how to harness the power of Python and its Pandas & matplotlib modules to visualize, explore, and analyse real world data sets. The workshop will be conducted in a computer lab, where you will be able to immediately apply the skills and techniques you will learn in the complementing lectures.

By the end of the workshop, not only will you be a better Python programmer, but will also be well on your way to tackling competitions on Kaggle - a tried and tested path to a career in Data Science.

AI Meetup #5

12th April 2018

CIS Lecture Hall, NED

The technical head, Ashba Jawed, general Secretary AIC started the meetup in full energy, explaining about the great revolution of AI. This meetup included two great sessions on ANN(Artificial Neural Networks) & CNNs(Convolutional Neural Networks). The first speaker, M.Hassaan Anwar, explained ANNs in an amazing manner and made ANNs look easy to the audience. He described that an ANN is a model based on the structure of biological neural networks. ANN takes data samples rather than entire data sets to arrive at solutions, which saves both time and money.

The second speaker, Shahmir Khan, explained CNNs, their layers and their examples. He discussed how an image is a matrix of pixel values that can be manipulated by a CNN, whose neurones are arranged in 3 dimensions: width, height, no. of layers. A CNN is a sequence of layers, and every layer of a CNN transforms one volume of activations to another through a differentiable function. Three main types of layers to build CNN’s architecture: Convolutional Layer, Pooling Layer, and Fully-Connected Layer. The Convolutional layer is the core building block of a Convolutional Network that does most of the computational heavy lifting. The Pooling Layer does the tuning of the image

Data Science Workshop

7th February 2018

Civil AV Hall, NEDUET

AI Club hosted its first Data Science Workshop on February 6th, 2018 which turned out to be an inspiring event for Data Scientists and innovators alike. The theme of the event was to guide students on what is data science, its applications, job opportunities in Pakistan and how to get started on Kaggle, which is a world-renowned website that hosts hundreds of top-notch data science competitions.
Special thanks to our guest speakers:

Khurram Hussain - CEO, Mojo Solutions who gave an overview of Data Science and its current importance, meanwhile highlighting the courses students should pursue to start their career in Data Sciences.

Noman Khalid - CEO, Love for Data explored the subject more rigorously, outlining intricate details.

And Junaid Effendi, Data Scientist, CIS Alumni: pointing out some of the most common mistakes of novice Data Scientists.

AI Club would also like to thank Dr. Muhammad Khurram - Head of Research Centre for Artificial Intelligence for providing AI Club the platform to organize such events. Dr Khurram was our chief guest for the event.

Team AI Club is proud to organize such events with data enthusiasts and technocrats in NED and aims to take Artificial Intelligence to a newer level in Pakistan.

In the end of the event, AIC gifted Usman Abbas a “Google Home device” for being the first student at NED to complete Coursera’s Deep Learning specialization by Andrew Ng. We are proud to have motivated students like Usman at our club.
